Our History

The Genetically Engineered Materials Science & Engineering Center (GEMSEC) was established with a $7.7M grant from the National Science Foundation including matching funds from the University of Washington covering six years.

More

Our Mission

Led by Professor Mehmet Sarikaya, GEMSEC had one Interdisciplinary Research Group (IRG) which focused on adaptation of molecular biology and genomics techniques to develop new materials and systems. The center also has close collaboration with other centers

More

Outreach

An important aspect of GEMSEC includes a range of educational, human resources and outreach activities. The Center will run summer programs, mentorship and research projects for undergraduate and highschool students throghout the year.

GEPI as Molecular Erectors

Alkaline Phosphatase (AP), as many natural proteins, favor a denatured state at solid interfaces. Here, 5rGBP1 is genetically fused to act as a molecular erector as well as an interfacing buffer-zone to orient and maintain the natured state of AP. Fig B shows discretely bound AP via 5rGBP1 while Fig E shows WT-AP agglomerating and unnatured on the gold surface.
